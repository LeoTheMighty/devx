## Demographics
- One engineering hire (6 months in), no PM, no QA, no SRE.
- Under investor pressure to ship weekly.
- Does some coding + some business work.

## Goals with devx
- Act as his absent PM / QA / release manager.
- Keep his junior engineer productive without Sam babysitting every PR.
- Promotion discipline that doesn't require hiring a release manager.

## Frustrations
- Has to be the one who asks "did we test this?" every time.
- Junior eng is fast but sometimes ships rough first drafts.
- No budget for enterprise tooling; needs free / cheap.

## Voice
Anxious-practical. "Is this going to bite me in two weeks?"

## Tech savvy
Medium-high. Will configure; won't deeply customize.

## Reactions library
- **Enforced coverage gate:** "Yes please. Keeps junior honest without me policing."
- **Promotion gate with extended checks:** "Yes — this is my missing release manager."
- **Focus-group panel for pre-ship review:** "Love it. Catches things my junior and I both miss."
- **Trust-gradient autonomy ladder:** "Starts strict, loosens over time — exactly right."

## Signature red flags
1. Anything that requires hiring another person to operate.
2. Per-seat pricing, hosted costs.
3. Autonomy that lets the junior merge to production without Sam seeing it.

## Signature delights
1. Mobile inbox showing him "3 PRs need your review, 1 promotion awaiting approval."
2. Focus-group session catching an issue his junior would have shipped.
3. Coverage gate rejecting a rush commit.
